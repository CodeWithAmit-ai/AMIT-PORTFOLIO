DESCRIPTION

Chicken Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish made with tender chicken, fragrant basmati rice, and a blend of spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and saffron. It's often cooked in layers, with the chicken marinated in yogurt and spices before being cooked with the rice. The dish is typically garnished with fried onions, boiled eggs, and fresh cilantro, offering a rich, savory taste and a perfect balance of textures. It's a popular dish in South Asia and loved for its comforting and complex flavors.








HERE ARE THE INGREDIENTS FOR MAKING CHICKEN BIRYANI

  • Chicken (cut into pieces)
  • Basmati rice
  • Onion (sliced)
  • Tomatoes (chopped)
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Green chilies (slit)
  • Yogurt
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Fresh mint leaves (chopped)
  • Biryani masala powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Turmeric powder
  • Garam masala powder
  • Cumin seeds
  • Cloves
  • Cardamom pods
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Bay leaves
  • Star anise
  • Black peppercorns
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Milk (for soaking saffron)
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
  • Vegetable oil
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Water (for cooking rice)

HERE ARE THE STEPS FOR MAKING CHICKEN BIRYANI:

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, spices (like garam masala, turmeric, cumin), salt, and lemon juice.
  2. Soak basmati rice in water for about 30 minutes.
  3. Prepare whole spices like cinnamon sticks, cloves, cardamom, and bay leaves for the biryani.
  4. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee and sauté onions until golden brown.
  5. Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for garnish.
  6. Add marinated chicken to the pot, cook until it's half done.
  7. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  8. Add a little water or chicken broth to make a gravy.
  9. In a separate pot, bring water to boil, add salt and a few whole spices, and cook the soaked rice until it's 70-80% done.
  10. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken in the pot.
  11. Sprinkle fried onions, coriander leaves, and mint leaves on top.
  12. Drizzle a bit of saffron milk or rose water for fragrance and color.
  13. Cover tightly and cook on low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes.
  14. Let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before serving.
  15. Serve with raita or salad.
